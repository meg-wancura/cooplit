Academia: Unnecessarily Chaotic

Cooplit is a project primarily by and for graduate students focused on making academia more navigable.

And therefore more equitable!

Cooplit started out as a funny idea one day while looking at the 500 papers in my Endnote library and texting a crush (and therefore thinking about my many, many experiences with dating apps)
After realizing that one couldn't simply post: "please make this thing" online and have it done according to their whims, I decided to try to make it myself! Of course, with the help of others. The vision has evolved greatly from this first text as I went from Chemistry Ph.D. student with a lot of teaching experience to Chemistry Ph.D. student with a lot of teaching experience and one business class.
Cooplit stands for "Cooperative Literature". The basic premise is a strong belief that one would have a lot to say to those who had a similar body of literature in their library. Cooplit is for all the major nerds (like me) who want desperately to talk to someone who understands their research at a deep level [[[other than their professor]]].
In order to acheive a network that enables one to find another with similar literature interests, a vision of a web of academia appeared. One where an outsider could see "who does what where" in academia. One where someone who wasn't a major nerd could ask

Academia: Unnecessarily (Emotionally) Painful

Cooplit is a project primarily by and for graduate students focused on making academia more navigable.

We also care deeply about empowering graduate students to have the best possible experience navigating the difficulties of academia. This project aims to ensure graduate students can more easily navigate the complicated networks of academia, establishing their own network beyond that which exists ofr their PI.
